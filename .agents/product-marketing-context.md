# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-31*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** FenceFind is a contractor directory that helps homeowners find trusted fence installers in their area.
**What it does:** FenceFind connects homeowners with top-rated fence contractors across 500+ US cities. Homeowners search by location, compare contractors by ratings and reviews, and request free estimates — all at no cost. Contractors get a free listing and can upgrade to Pro ($199/mo) for exclusive leads, featured placement, and lead notifications.
**Product category:** Contractor directory / home services marketplace
**Product type:** Two-sided marketplace (freemium for contractors, free for homeowners)
**Business model:** Contractor subscriptions — $199/month Pro plan (14-day free trial, cancel anytime). Homeowners use the platform for free.

## Target Audience

### Homeowners (Demand Side)
**Who:** Homeowners actively researching fence installation or repair
**Primary use case:** Finding and comparing local fence contractors to get estimates
**Jobs to be done:**
- Find a trustworthy fence contractor near me without calling around
- Compare ratings, reviews, and services before reaching out
- Get free estimates without commitment or sales pressure

**Use cases:**
- New fence installation (privacy, security, pets, kids, property line)
- Fence repair or replacement
- Comparing materials and costs before committing
- Verifying a contractor's credentials before hiring

### Contractors (Supply Side / Revenue)
**Who:** Fence installation and repair businesses — typically small-to-mid local operations (1-20 employees)
**Decision-makers:** Business owner or general manager
**Primary use case:** Getting a steady stream of qualified local leads without per-lead fees or bidding wars
**Jobs to be done:**
- Get more fence installation and repair jobs in my service area
- Stop wasting money on shared leads and pay-per-lead platforms
- Show up when local homeowners are searching for fence contractors

**Use cases:**
- New contractor building a customer base
- Established contractor diversifying lead sources beyond word-of-mouth
- Contractor frustrated with HomeAdvisor/Angi lead quality and cost

## Problems & Pain Points

### Homeowners
**Core problem:** Finding a reliable fence contractor is a pain — you're stuck Googling, reading scattered reviews, and calling around hoping someone picks up.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Google Maps is noisy — hard to tell who's legit vs. who bought fake reviews
- HomeAdvisor/Angi push you into a funnel where contractors are bidding for your attention (not your best interest)
- Asking neighbors only works if someone nearby recently got a fence
**What it costs them:** Weeks of research, risk of hiring a bad contractor, overpaying, project delays
**Emotional tension:** Fear of getting ripped off, overwhelm from too many options with no way to compare, anxiety about a major home investment

### Contractors
**Core problem:** Lead generation platforms eat into margins with shared leads, per-lead fees, and bidding wars — and the lead quality is inconsistent.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- HomeAdvisor/Angi sell the same lead to 3-5 contractors, turning it into a race to the bottom
- Per-lead pricing means you pay whether or not the lead converts
- Broad platforms lump fence contractors in with plumbers, electricians, and roofers — no specialization
**What it costs them:** Wasted ad spend, time chasing dead leads, unpredictable monthly costs
**Emotional tension:** Frustration with paying for leads that go nowhere, feeling like a commodity, uncertainty about ROI

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** HomeAdvisor / Angi — falls short because they sell shared leads to multiple contractors, charge per-lead fees, and cover every trade (no fence specialization). Contractors feel like they're bidding against each other.
**Secondary:** Google Business Profile / Google Maps — falls short because there's no curation, no lead routing, and contractors can't differentiate beyond basic reviews. Homeowners have to do all the legwork.
**Indirect:** Word-of-mouth / Nextdoor referrals — falls short because it's not scalable, limited to who you know, and contractors can't proactively generate leads this way.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Fence-only vertical: 100% focused on fence contractors (not a generalist platform)
- No shared leads: Every lead in your service area goes exclusively to you
- Flat-rate pricing: $199/mo with no per-lead fees, no bidding wars, no surprises
- 14-day free trial: No credit card required to start
- Transparent and simple: Cancel anytime, no contracts, no hidden fees

**How we do it differently:** Instead of auctioning leads to the highest bidder, FenceFind gives Pro contractors every lead in their service area for one flat monthly rate. One job pays for a full year.
**Why that's better:** Contractors know exactly what they're paying, get exclusive leads, and don't compete against other contractors on the platform for the same customer.
**Why customers choose us:** Predictable cost, higher quality leads (homeowners actively searching for fence contractors specifically), and no competition for leads within their area.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Frustration with paying for shared leads that don't convert. Unpredictable monthly lead gen costs. Feeling like one of many on generalist platforms.
**Pull:** Exclusive leads, flat-rate pricing, fence-specific audience, free trial to test it risk-free, "one job pays for a full year" ROI math.
**Habit:** Familiarity with HomeAdvisor/Angi. "It's what I've always used." Already paying and don't want to manage another platform.
**Anxiety:** "Will I actually get leads?" "Is the volume enough to justify the cost?" "Is this just another platform that won't deliver?"
