**Full content audit completed across all page types:**
- City pages (~500 words) — too thin to rank, only 3 FAQs, main traffic driver but weakest content
- State pages (~300 words) — no schema, no FAQs, no state-specific content at all
- Fence cost pages (~1500 words) — decent but templated across cities
- Fence permit pages (~2000 words) — strongest content, unique differentiator vs competitors
- Guides (~2000+ words each, 11 total) — strong but underlinked from homepage
- Blog — only 3 posts, one duplicates a guide topic
- Homepage — doesn't link to guides or blog

**Keyword research findings:**
- 1.1M+ monthly fence-related searches in US
- Best opportunity: city + cost long-tail keywords (low competition, high intent)
- Permit pages are a real differentiator — Angi/HomeAdvisor don't cover this well
- DIY keywords are massive volume (10-15K/mo) and convert to "hire a pro"
- "Near me" head terms too competitive for now — need domain authority first
- Competitive advantage: fence-only vertical specialization vs Angi's 500-category spread

**Internal linking gaps identified:**
- Homepage doesn't link to guides or blog
- Guides don't link back to city pages (one-way only)
- State pages don't link to guides or blog
- No FAQ schema on homepage
